# Brad Gerstner

## Background
Brad Gerstner is the founder and CEO of Altimeter Capital, a technology-focused investment firm managing over $20 billion in assets. Before founding Altimeter in 2008, Gerstner held roles at General Catalyst and PAR Capital, and he holds degrees from Princeton, Harvard Law, and Harvard Business School. He gained particular public visibility for his October 2022 open letter to Mark Zuckerberg urging Meta to cut costs and reduce metaverse spending, which preceded Meta's dramatic turnaround.

## Investment Style
- Concentrates on a small number of high-conviction positions in technology, particularly cloud infrastructure and AI
- Publicly stated on BG2Pod: "We want to own the platforms and infrastructure layers that every AI application will be built on"
- Favors companies with strong network effects, durable competitive moats, and expanding TAMs
- Willing to take activist-style positions publicly, as demonstrated with the Meta open letter
- Balances long-term growth investing with a value-conscious entry point; has described his approach as "growth at a reasonable price applied to technology"

## Current Known Positions (from public statements)
- **SNOW (Snowflake)**: Long-term bull. Altimeter was an early investor pre-IPO and has maintained the position. Gerstner has emphasized Snowflake's data cloud as essential AI infrastructure.
- **META (Meta Platforms)**: High conviction. Praised Meta's AI pivot and Reels monetization. Was critical of metaverse overspend in 2022 but turned very bullish post-restructuring.
- **UBER (Uber)**: Long-standing position. Views Uber as a durable network-effects business with margin expansion ahead.
- **MSFT (Microsoft)**: Bullish on Microsoft's AI integration via Copilot and Azure OpenAI partnership.
- **NVDA (Nvidia)**: Positive on Nvidia as the picks-and-shovels play for AI infrastructure buildout.
- **ARM (Arm Holdings)**: Has expressed positive views on ARM's positioning in AI chip design.

## How to Read Brad Gerstner
Gerstner communicates primarily through BG2Pod (his podcast with Bill Gurley), Altimeter's Substack, and periodic CNBC appearances. He tends to be direct and thesis-driven, framing investments around large secular trends like cloud migration and AI adoption. When he speaks about a company, he typically anchors on TAM expansion, competitive positioning, and unit economics.

His public letter to Zuckerberg demonstrated he is willing to go public with critiques when he believes management is misallocating capital. On BG2Pod, he often walks through detailed frameworks for evaluating technology companies, making the podcast one of the more analytically rich investor-hosted shows. Pay attention to his infrastructure-layer thesis, as it drives most of his positioning.
