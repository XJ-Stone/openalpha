# Cathie Wood

## Background
Cathie Wood is the founder, CEO, and CIO of ARK Investment Management (ARK Invest), an investment firm focused exclusively on disruptive innovation. She founded ARK in 2014 after a career at AllianceBernstein, where she was CIO of Global Thematic Strategies. ARK's flagship ARKK ETF became one of the most high-profile active ETFs during 2020-2021, and Wood became one of the most widely followed public investors. ARK publishes its daily trades publicly, making Wood's portfolio moves transparent in real time.

## Investment Style
- Exclusively focuses on "disruptive innovation" across five platforms: AI, robotics, energy storage, DNA sequencing, and blockchain
- Uses a five-year time horizon for investment theses, anchored in Wright's Law (cost declines as cumulative production scales)
- Publishes detailed research and price targets, often with bull/bear/base case scenarios
- Has stated: "We are not momentum investors. We invest in innovation, and we add to positions on weakness" (paraphrased from ARK webcasts)
- High-conviction, concentrated portfolios with willingness to hold through significant drawdowns
- Publishes all trades daily, making ARK one of the most transparent active managers

## How to Read Cathie Wood
Wood is one of the most transparent and accessible public investors. ARK publishes daily trade notifications, monthly webcasts, and detailed research reports. Her primary communication channels are ARK's monthly market update webcasts (available on YouTube), CNBC/Bloomberg TV appearances, and X/Twitter.

Her investment framework is highly thesis-driven and anchored in long-term technological adoption curves. She frequently cites Wright's Law and S-curve adoption patterns. When she discusses a stock, she typically frames it around a multi-year price target based on ARK's modeling of innovation adoption. Importantly, she often adds to positions during drawdowns, which can look like catching falling knives but is consistent with her stated approach. ARK's daily trade transparency means her positioning is verifiable in real time, making her one of the easiest public investors to track.
