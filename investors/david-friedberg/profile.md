# David Friedberg

## Background
David Friedberg is the founder and CEO of The Production Board, a holding company and venture fund focused on using technology to address fundamental challenges in food production, agriculture, climate, and healthcare. Before founding The Production Board, Friedberg founded The Climate Corporation, a weather data analytics company that Monsanto acquired for approximately $1.1 billion in 2013. He previously worked at Google. He is a co-host of the All-In Podcast, where he provides a science and technology-grounded perspective.

## Investment Style
- Focuses on deep technology companies solving fundamental challenges in food, agriculture, climate, energy, and healthcare
- Data-driven and scientific approach — frequently references research papers and first-principles analysis on All-In
- Invests in companies where technology can unlock order-of-magnitude improvements in efficiency or cost
- Paraphrased from All-In: "I want to invest in companies where the science is clear but the market hasn't caught up to the implications"
- More focused on private company investing through The Production Board, but provides public market commentary on All-In
- Tends to evaluate AI through the lens of specific applications (agriculture, biology, materials science) rather than pure infrastructure

## Current Known Positions (from public statements)
- **TSLA (Tesla)**: Has discussed Tesla on All-In, particularly the energy storage and solar business, which aligns with his climate technology focus.
- **GOOG (Alphabet)**: Has discussed Google's DeepMind and its applications in scientific research (protein folding, weather prediction, materials science).
- **AMZN (Amazon)**: Has discussed Amazon's logistics and technology infrastructure.

Note: Friedberg's primary investments are through The Production Board in private companies. His public market commentary on All-In focuses more on thematic analysis than specific stock picks.

## How to Read David Friedberg
Friedberg's value on the All-In Podcast is his deep scientific and technological literacy. While the other hosts often focus on financial markets and macro, Friedberg brings a perspective grounded in biology, chemistry, physics, and environmental science. When he discusses a technology or investment theme, he often goes deeper into the underlying science than any other host.

His commentary is most valuable when evaluating AI applications in specific domains (agriculture, biology, climate), assessing the scientific plausibility of technology claims, and understanding second-order effects of AI on physical-world industries. He tends to be less focused on near-term stock price movements and more on multi-decade technology adoption curves. When Friedberg gets excited about a scientific breakthrough, it is worth paying attention, as his track record at The Climate Corporation demonstrates his ability to identify transformative applications of data science.
