# David Sacks

## Background
David Sacks is a General Partner at Craft Ventures, a venture capital firm he founded in 2017. He was the founding COO of PayPal (part of the "PayPal Mafia") and later founded and served as CEO of Yammer, which Microsoft acquired for $1.2 billion in 2012. In December 2024, he was named the White House AI & Crypto Czar by President Trump, a role focused on shaping U.S. policy on artificial intelligence and digital assets. He is a co-host of the All-In Podcast.

## Investment Style
- Enterprise software-first investor with deep product intuition from building Yammer and PayPal
- Emphasizes the importance of product-market fit and efficient growth (not growth-at-all-costs)
- Early and vocal proponent of AI as transformative for enterprise software, particularly in coding, customer support, and data analysis
- Has argued that AI will compress the time from startup to scale, favoring lean, AI-native companies
- Increasingly focused on defense tech and government technology applications

## Current Known Positions (from public statements)
- **NVDA (Nvidia)**: Bullish. Has discussed Nvidia as essential AI infrastructure on multiple All-In episodes.
- **PLTR (Palantir)**: Positive on Palantir's government and defense AI positioning. Has noted the company's unique ability to deploy AI in classified and sensitive environments.
- **MSFT (Microsoft)**: Views Microsoft as the enterprise AI distribution leader through Copilot and Azure.
- **GOOG (Alphabet)**: Has discussed Google's AI capabilities but expressed concern about the company's execution speed relative to competitors.

## How to Read David Sacks
Sacks communicates primarily through the All-In Podcast and occasionally through X/Twitter posts. His analysis tends to be product-focused and draws heavily on his operating experience at PayPal and Yammer. He frames technology trends through the lens of enterprise adoption and product-market fit rather than pure financial metrics.

Following his appointment as White House AI & Crypto Czar in late 2024, his public commentary has shifted to include more policy perspectives on AI regulation, export controls, and the competitive dynamics between U.S. and Chinese AI development. His views on AI policy now carry direct policy implications, making his All-In commentary particularly relevant for investors tracking regulatory risk.
