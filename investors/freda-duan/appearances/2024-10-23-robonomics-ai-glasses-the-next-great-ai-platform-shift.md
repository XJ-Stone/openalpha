# Robonomics — October 23, 2024

Glasses are shaping up to be the next big disruptive form factor in AI.

The global eyewear market is massive—$500B+, with over 4 billion people wearing glasses. And now, after years of false starts, we're finally seeing traction.

📈 Rumors suggest $Meta-RayBan glasses have already sold over 1 million units.

(For context: the first iPhone sold 6M units over its entire lifespan.)

Why Glasses, Why Now?

Glasses offer unique advantages for AI that smartphones can’t match:

Full-sensory context—the assistant can see and hear what you do.

First-person perspective—perfect for ambient computing.

Hands-free, continuous capture—seamless and unobtrusive.

Rich sensor input—head motion, gaze direction, immediate surroundings.

And crucially: AI hardware is never just about the device—it’s about the OS behind it.

Orion: A Glimpse Into the Future

Meta’s Orion glasses showcase key hardware and UX breakthroughs:

Fully standalone—no smartphone required

Wireless, lightweight, and visually indistinguishable from regular glasses

Built-in AI assistant integrated into Meta’s messaging ecosystem

This has been Zuck’s long-term vision: holographic glasses as the gateway to the Metaverse. While replacing smartphones is still years away, Orion is a meaningful first step toward that goal.

📌 Why Ray-Ban Meta Succeeded Where Others Failed

Meta’s first attempt—Ray-Ban Stories (2021)—flopped:

Sold just 300k units

<10% monthly active users

High return rates

Yet the Ray-Ban Meta (2023)—despite a similar form factor—is a hit. Why?

AI progress in 2023 has primed the market for intelligent assistants in new form factors. It’s no longer about novelty—it’s about utility.

💀 A Graveyard of Past Attempts

$GOOGL Glass (2013)

$1,500, no partners, <100k units. Killed in under a year.

Market wasn’t ready—privacy & safety concerns.

$SNAP Spectacles (2016)

<300k sold, $40M loss. Limited to the Snap app.

Great idea, poor integration. Overstock disaster.

What’s Different This Time?

AI Integration

Everyone wants an assistant. Meta is offering one—with eyes and ears.

Meta’s Ecosystem Lock-in

As @boztank said:

“With WhatsApp and Messenger, people can access Meta AI. But you can’t do that through your AirPods.”

Final Thought:

@boztank summed it up perfectly:

“Breakthroughs tend to accumulate in a non-linear way—until suddenly, the future comes into focus. It happens two ways: gradually and then suddenly.”

We may be living through that “suddenly” moment for wearable AI.
