# Robonomics — October 29, 2024

Opex Efficiency in Exchange for Capex Spend?

Why Trade Opex for Capex?

AI’s compute demands are scaling aggressively. As $MSFT’s Satya Nadella pointed out, maintaining performance improvements requires ~4x more compute (FLOPS) each year. Between GPT-4 and GPT-5 (over two years), the number of training chips jumped 5x—from 8k to 40k—and training duration expanded from 1 to 5 months.

Even the best-capitalized firms can’t afford 4x cost growth forever. But the narrative is shifting.

[IMAGE: Image]

$GOOGL, on its 3Q earnings call, said it expects to “boost capex next year but drive efficiencies to offset part of this.”

AI for Efficiency, Not Just Cost-Cutting

Unlike the early 2023 wave of layoffs, AI is now being deployed to increase productivity rather than reduce headcount.

$NVDA: Jensen plans to scale from 32k to 50k employees, supported by 100M AI agents. This implies a powerful AI-assisted operating model—higher productivity and higher headcount.

$V: Visa is evolving roles to better serve clients. Headcount may still grow, but the shape of the workforce changes.

$GOOGL: “25% of new code is AI-generated.” That’s a massive efficiency tailwind.

Opex vs. Capex: A New Framework?

$GOOGL also said:

“Hopefully, we’ll drive efficiencies to offset some or all of that capex increase.”

To me, that reads like a trade-off: opex savings helping fund rising capex. The Street currently estimates:

FY24 opex: $92B

FY25 opex: $100B

But here’s the question—why does opex need to grow at all? Holding opex flat could free up billions, yielding ~200bps of margin leverage while preserving headroom for aggressive AI investment.

FCF Becomes More Durable

I’ve been calculating each company’s “max FCF” to understand how long their current capex trajectory is sustainable. With AI-driven opex efficiency, FCF expands, extending the AI capex runway.

This could stretch the AI investment cycle by a few more years—giving companies more time to realize ROI and keep scaling.
