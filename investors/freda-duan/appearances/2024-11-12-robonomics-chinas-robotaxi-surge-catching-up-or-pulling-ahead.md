# Robonomics — November 12, 2024

China's Robotaxi Status Check (vs. the West)

Globally, consumers want cheaper, safer transportation. It’s notable that both $Waymo and $BIDU have reached utilization rates that rival traditional ride-hailing services.

The traditional model is being disrupted—$BIDU alone is targeting 30% of the taxi and ride-hailing market by 2030.

Self-driving is a massive market with room for multiple winners. $TSLA, acting as a "catfish," may help accelerate global adoption.

▶️ BOM Will Drop Significantly

I was shocked to see $BIDU’s RT6 priced at just US$28k (RMB 200k, including lidars!), which is 50–60% cheaper than RT5.

Lidar costs have plummeted 90%+ over the last four years to ~$500/unit. Even with 7 units (as in Pony.ai’s case), the lidar BOM is just ~$3,000.

$Waymo’s Hyundai partnership should also help slash BOM significantly.

[IMAGE: Image]

▶️ Pricing Will Undercut Ride-Hailing

Self-driving rides can be 20–40% cheaper than traditional ride-hailing, easily.

$BIDU is already pricing rides 50–70% lower than $DIDI to acquire users—and still guiding toward breakeven by FY25 with a 40% discount.

$Waymo currently charges at parity with $Uber/$Lyft, but could cut prices 20–40% and remain profitable as scale improves.

▶️ Fleet Ramp-Up Could Surprise to the Upside

$Waymo and $BIDU each operate ~1,000 cars today. Both $BIDU and $Pony.ai are targeting 40,000 vehicles (!) by 2030.

$Waymo’s Hyundai partnership should accelerate fleet scaling.

All L4 players are targeting major metros. If $BIDU can handle highways already, there’s no reason $Waymo can’t do the same.

▶️ Unit Economics

Breakeven (in select cities) is the near-term goal across the board.

$Waymo may already be breakeven in SF. $BIDU is guiding for breakeven by FY25.

$BIDU estimates needing ~3,000–5,000 cars per city to hit breakeven.

▶️ Safety Drivers: A Gray Area with “Dark Secrets”

There’s speculation some robotaxis are being remotely teleoperated full-time. The truth is likely somewhere in the middle—safety drivers still play a role.

Driver-to-car ratios today:

$BIDU: 2–3

Pony.ai: ~5

$Waymo: likely ~5

Long-term, these ratios must improve for unit economics to work.

▶️ Operations: The Problem Nobody Wants

Globally, L4 firms aim to offload ops—everyone dreams of an “asset-light” model. But execution remains a long-term pain point.

▶️ Charging vs. Battery Swaps

$BIDU’s RT6 uses battery swaps—a smart move that may cut down labor tied to charging.

▶️ Burn Rates: China Is Leaner

$BIDU spends ~US$700M/year on self-driving (per sell-side estimates).

By comparison:

$Waymo (Alphabet "Other Bets") likely burns several billion

Cruise is burning ~$2B/year, with $7–8B in total losses

▶️ Regulatory Fragmentation Slows Everyone

China’s approval process is city-by-city with no central playbook—mirroring U.S. fragmentation. A true federal framework would be a game-changer for scale.
