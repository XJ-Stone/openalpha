# Robonomics — November 21, 2024

Since my last update, Sequoia and Goldman Sachs have echoed growing concerns around the AI trade: too much capex, too little revenue.

These worries—combined with stretched positioning in semis, macro risks (Taiwan, Trump, rate cuts, war, elections...) and a growing debate over ROI—have triggered sharp market pullbacks.

This isn’t the first AI reset we've seen. There were:

H2 2023 concerns around the "$NVDA 2025 cliff"

Q2 2024 panic over a "H2 air pocket"

But something feels different this time.

[IMAGE: Image]

1⃣ Exponential Training Costs Are Catching Up

Model training costs are exploding—5x+ per generation.

That kind of growth is fine when capex is $1B...less fine when it's $50–100B.

Researchers often assume a blank check. Investors don’t.

2Q earnings helped calm nerves temporarily, as Big Tech raised not just 3Q/2H24 capex, but CY25 budgets as well. The real question is 2026—can this pace continue?

2⃣ The AI Oligopoly Has Formed

Just a few months ago, there were dozens of model labs.

Now?

$MSFT → Inflection

$AMZN → Adept

$GOOGL → Character

$META, $XAI building in-house

Four players now dominate model training.

That’s dangerous in any capital-intensive sector.

If one blinks—due to ROI pressure, regulatory scrutiny, or internal shifts—it could cascade across the stack.

3⃣ Training vs. Inference Divergence

The biggest 2024 trend? The rise of small models:

GPT-4o (90% cheaper than GPT-4)

Gemini 1.5

Llama3 8B

GPT-4o-mini, Claude 3 Haiku

While $NVDA’s Jensen says ~40% of usage is inference (driven by $META), capex remains training-heavy. The dominant strategy today: train a massive model → distill it for inference.

This divergence—falling inference cost vs. rising training cost—is unsustainable at scale.

▶️ AI Revenue ≠ the Problem

Total “trackable” AI software/API revenue is ~$20B.

That may sound small—but consider:

ChatGPT launched less than 2 years ago

Most revenue is still API-based and intermittent

Value created by AI far exceeds direct API billings

This is not a revenue problem—it’s a capex control problem.

Final Take

We're still early in a multi-year AI supercycle.

But capital markets are not infinite.

The trade doesn’t end overnight—but it will continue to whiplash between euphoria and doubt, especially as investors try to make sense of unprecedented capex vs. still-maturing monetization.

GPT-5 isn’t even out yet.

The real test of AI ROI is just beginning.
