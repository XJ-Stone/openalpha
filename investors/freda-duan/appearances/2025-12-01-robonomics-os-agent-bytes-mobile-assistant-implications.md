# Robonomics — December 01, 2025

Doubao Mobile Assistant - ByteDance just showed the strongest OS-level agent I’ve seen

Video: https://x.com/dermotmcg/status/1995378039673483491?s=20

ByteDance just dropped Doubao Mobile Assistant, and it showcased some of the most interesting OS-agent use cases so far. Big implications for AI use cases, phone market share, and the iOS vs Android divergence.

The key idea: “operating-system-level collaboration between Doubao and mobile phone manufacturers.”

Meaning: this is not a standalone app. It is a system service baked into the ROM, able to drive the UI like a human across apps, mostly via real-time voice.

What it can do

1. Cross-app commerce and bookings

Example:

“Find which KFC spicy chicken burger is cheaper on JD vs Meituan vs Taobao, pick the lowest price, deliver to X address, add note ‘leave at front desk,’ then screenshot the order and send to A on WeChat.”

The agent literally opens and navigates multiple shopping apps, compares prices, and completes the entire workflow.

2. Productivity and “phone secretary” tasks

Auto-replies in chat apps, forwarding files, sharing links, managing repetitive cross-app chores.

3. Media, photos, and creative tasks

Voice-driven edits, generation, and multi-step actions across apps.

Thoughts and predictions

1. Byte as a phone player

Byte (imho China’s OpenAI) is effectively entering the phone market.

Other Android brands in China (vivo, OPPO, Honor, etc.) control ~53 percent of the market. All of that is now potentially up for grabs if “Byte phone” becomes the flagship agent-first experience.

2. iOS vs Android

This level of full-screen GUI driving requires ROM-level privileges that Android OEMs can grant.

iOS is far more locked down. Siri will always be the first-class, tightly controlled agent.

The long-term trajectory of the two ecosystems is suddenly unclear.

3. ’s path

Apple partnered with OpenAI and Gemini, but an OS agent at this depth has to be in-house. Medium to long term, Apple must build a native agent at the system level.

4. Pixel

Google looks like a major winner (again!). Pixel is the most vertically integrated Android stack, and this kind of agent fits their strategy perfectly.

5. China vs the US

China is different:

A more complete walled-garden app ecosystem - an OS-level agent disrupts all apps but is necessary if you want the agent to actually be useful.

In the US, it is extremely hard to introduce a new hardware player - iPhone’s hardware moat is still enormous. The open question: can anyone (OpenAI) succesfully make a new hardware while making the OS agent work?

6. Phones vs other form factors

This does not lock Byte into phones. They already launched earbuds and may pursue glasses.

By sitting at the OS level, they keep flexibility to move across hardware form factors - smart move imo, especially given the Chinese market is not short of hardware providers (hardware = pure commodity in China).

7. 2026 hardware shakeup

Expect meaningful market share shifts across the major players.

8. Voice as the primary interface

Old idea, but my conviction is rising. The future belongs to Voice-first agents.
