# Robonomics — December 16, 2025

Waymo GMV and scale - back-of-the-envelope

Assuming ~450k trips per week (Tiger Global investor letter) or ~3,000 cars on the road (Waymo official commentary), and ~$16 per trip (shorter than Uber trips, but comparable on a per-mile basis), Waymo’s current GMV run-rate is ~$350–400m.

If Waymo scales to 7–10k vehicles over the next 12–18 months (pure guessing; directional estimate), GMV scales to ~$1–2bn run-rate. At a rumored ~$100bn valuation, this implies an implied GMV multiple of ~50–100x.

Execution and expansion

Waymo’s execution to date has been strong. It is already fully autonomous in 10 cities and plans to enter 20+ additional cities in 2026, including Baltimore, Pittsburgh, St. Louis, Philadelphia, Minneapolis, New Orleans, Tampa, San Diego, Las Vegas, Detroit, Denver, Nashville, Seattle, Washington D.C., plus international markets such as London and Tokyo. Testing (manual or autonomous with specialists) is already underway in many of these markets. Public launch timing varies by city and regulatory approval, with several expected to open to riders in 2026.

Scale milestone

Waymo reports 14+ million trips in 2025 YTD, more than 3x last year’s public rides, putting the platform on track to exceed 20 million lifetime trips by year-end.

BOM costs:

5th Gen - Jaguar I-PACE

Waymo receives finished Jaguars, tears them down, and retrofits sensors and compute. This manual, post-production process adds significant labor and cost.

6th Gen - Hyundai Foundry Model

Waymo shifts to a true foundry model.

Hyundai builds vehicles on its Georgia assembly line specifically for Waymo.

Cars roll off autonomous-ready (redundant steering/braking, power doors, pre-wiring).

Sensors are installed on the line; Waymo just plugs in the “Driver.”

No teardown, no expensive retrofit labor.

What “6th Gen” Means

The 6th gen is not a car - it’s a vehicle-agnostic sensor + compute stack (first shown on Zeekr, now deployed across multiple platforms).

Waymo designs sensors in-house, driving ~90% cost reduction since 2009, with lidar costs still falling ~50% annually as solid-state matures.
