# Robonomics — December 19, 2025

If the numbers are even directionally right, prediction markets may already be at ~15–30% share of NFL betting during peak season.

4Q25 snapshot:

Kalshi + Polymarket reportedly doing ~$1.6B per week in sports volume

→ ~$21B over 4Q

[IMAGE: chart/figure]

Converting to traditional sportsbook handle (divide by ~4–8x)

→ $3–5B equivalent handle

Divide by 2x: Convert Kalshi notional to “handle-like cash put at risk

Divide by another 2-4x: Common sanity ranges: T = 2 (moderate churn); T = 4 (high churn)

[IMAGE: chart/figure]

For context:

NFL total handle ~ $30B annually

~60% happens in 4Q → ~$18B

[IMAGE: chart/figure]

That implies 15–30% market share during the quarter.

If this framing is right, it’s genuinely intriguing. A large chunk of activity likely comes from some 20 y/o users in CA / TX residents, where sports betting isn’t legalized.

Like it or not, betting taps into a deep human instinct - “I bet” is literally part of the language. The demand exists; the only open question is under what regulatory framework it gets expressed.

2026 should be even more interesting.

The World Cup in the US will be structurally larger than prior cycles:

39 days (vs. 29 days historically)

48 teams (vs. 32 teams historically)

104 matches (vs. 64 historically)

Hosted locally in the US!

Hard to pin down precisely, but $30B+ of US handle feels plausible.

With HOOD, COIN (and even DKNG, FLUT) all moving aggressively into prediction markets, the key question is no longer if—but how much share ultimately migrates to prediction markets.
