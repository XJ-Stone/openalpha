# Robonomics — January 16, 2026

It surprises me how underused connectors are. This is the feature that lets LLMs plug directly into calendars, inboxes, drives, and internal tools—and it’s where things stop being theoretical and start being useful. For consumers, connectors quietly redefine what “memory” means for AI. For enterprises, expect real adoption.

All three major LLM platforms now offer some version of this capability:

Claude: connectors

Gemini: Personal Intelligence

ChatGPT: enabled apps

The implementations differ, but the core integrations overlap heavily. Most already connect to:

Google Calendar

Gmail

Google Drive

Slack

And with those connections alone, the systems can already do a surprising amount of useful work:

“Look at my schedule for tomorrow—what conflicts do you see?”

“What subscriptions am I paying for, and when are they due?”

While they can’t yet send emails autonomously, they can locate the right contact, draft the message, and leave the final send action to the user

They can traverse shared work drives and pull relevant data on demand

Implications: 

For enterprises: expect significantly more adoption. Once LLMs can operate directly within existing workflows and data environments, the value proposition becomes much clearer.

For consumers: connectors collapse the memory problem. Instead of competing on who “remembers” you best, LLMs compete on access—leveling the playing field and shifting advantage to whoever integrates most cleanly.

For application ecosystems: APIs matter more than ever. Platforms that are easier to integrate with will win. Today, Google’s suite is far more accessible than Microsoft’s SharePoint and related tooling. If that gap persists, it’s reasonable to expect market share shifts driven not by features, but by integration friction.

AAPL Siri: With effectively leaning on Gemini for the upcoming Siri upgrade—and given that LLMs can already do all of the above—the bar is now clear. The new Siri should, at a minimum, match these capabilities (otherwise there is something srsly wrong with that team…)
