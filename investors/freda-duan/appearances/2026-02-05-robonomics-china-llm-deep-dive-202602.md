# Robonomics — February 05, 2026

State of the Market

Overall, the China LLM market looks far more fragmented than the US. Combined industry revenue is likely still sub–US$500M. Chinese consumers don’t pay - as has historically been the case. Enterprises also resist paying up for API pricing. A handful of model companies are targeting overseas markets, but taken together, this is not an easy market for monetization.

There is also no real “neo-labs” culture in China. You do see strong researchers leaving big platforms to start companies, but almost all of them are building AI applications rather than foundation-model-first labs.

On the model side, performance has been impressive. Public leaderboards increasingly show Chinese models at or near the global frontier.

2026 is shaping up to be the true year of competition for Chinese LLMs. The top consumer chatbots (Byte, Alibaba, Tencent) are rumored to be spending billions of USD on marketing.

Interestingly, very few teams talk about becoming “the Chinese OpenAI.” Instead, several want to be “the Chinese Anthropic.” Two reasons stand out: 1/ OpenAI’s emergence benefited from a rare alignment of timing, capital, and big-tech support. 2/ Chinese consumers simply do not pay for software, full stop.

Voice is a defining differentiator in China. Despite cultural stereotypes, voice likely accounts for ~1/3 of Doubao queries, driven by years of WeChat habituation and strong adoption among kids and the elderly, with low-latency, high-EQ voice models enabling entirely new usage patterns.

On the consumer side, Doubao (ByteDance) clearly dominates - ~100M DAU, with DAU/MAU around 30% (vs. ~15% for peers).

How Does It Compare With the US? - Financials

Valuation and multiples

Publicly cited numbers suggest Chinese LLMs are trading at significant premiums to US peers.

OpenAI / Anthropic: ~20–25x FY26E revenue, or ~10x FY27E.

Minimax: ~US$21B market cap, ~90x FY26E revenue, or ~40x FY27E (buyside consensus).

Zhipu: ~US$13B market cap, ~50–60x FY26E revenue, or ~35x FY27E (buyside consensus).

Kimi / Moonshot: ~US$5B valuation in a fresh round, per CNBC-sourced reporting (Jan 19, 2026).

Margins and burn

On publicly cited financials, US peers are running roughly a 1:1 burn-to-revenue ratio. In contrast, Minimax and Zhipu appear closer to 10:1 - frankly much higher than I expected.

Gross margins for Chinese players are currently lower (roughly 30–50%) versus US peers, though long-term expectations converge around ~70% GPM for everyone.

How Does It Compare With the US? - Products and User Experience

The most notable difference is voice usage. I don’t have hard numbers, but I suspect ~1/3 of Doubao queries are voice-based.

Kids walk around using Doubao’s “call me and talk” feature. Elderly users rely heavily on voice input and output. Voice quality is high, with low latency.

This is particularly interesting given that Chinese culture is typically more reserved. The likely explanation is WeChat. Roughly ~20% of WeChat messages are voice. Since a 60-second voice message carries far more information than text, voice may represent closer to ~50% of total information exchanged.

Video call features are also surprisingly creative. Parents use them for after-school “AI babysitting” - reminding kids to sit still, drink water, and stay focused.

Chinese LLMs, especially Doubao, generally show high EQ. Other features (video generation, image generation, PPT creation) are broadly on par with global peers.

On the enterprise side, adoption is largely top-down. SOEs are encouraged or required to integrate AI, usually through private deployments.
