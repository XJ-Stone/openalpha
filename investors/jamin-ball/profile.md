# Jamin Ball

## Background
Jamin Ball is a Partner at Altimeter Capital and author of Clouded Judgement, a widely-followed weekly Substack on SaaS and cloud software. He focuses on scaling enterprise and data infrastructure startups. He sits on the boards of Airbyte, ClickHouse, dbt Labs, Prisma, and Tabular, and has led investments in Deel, MotherDuck, Personio, and Starburst. He publishes weekly updates on public SaaS/cloud multiples alongside thematic analysis.

## Investment Style
- Metrics-first approach: publishes a weekly table of SaaS/cloud trading multiples (EV/Rev, FCF margins, Rule of 40)
- Deep focus on unit economics and free-cash-flow generation, even for high-growth software
- Evaluates private companies through the lens of public market comparables
- Tracks the intersection of open-source and commercial SaaS models
- Analyzes AI's impact on software business models — distinguishing between AI as tailwind vs. disruption to incumbent SaaS
- Frameworks tend to focus on durable growth, margin expansion, and capital efficiency

## Current Known Positions (from public statements)
- **Cloud/SaaS broadly**: Tracks the sector weekly with detailed multiple analysis. Views cloud infrastructure spend as a durable secular trend.
- **AI infrastructure**: Bullish on the data layer — portfolio includes ClickHouse, dbt Labs, MotherDuck, Tabular (all data infrastructure plays).
- **Software resilience**: Has argued that reports of "software's death" due to AI are premature; systems of record retain durable value.

## How to Read Jamin Ball
Ball's Clouded Judgement follows a consistent weekly format: a thematic essay on a SaaS/cloud topic followed by his signature multiples table tracking ~70 public software companies. The thematic essays reveal his current mental models — pay attention to recurring frameworks (Rule of 40, margin trajectories, build-vs-buy). His multiples data provides objective market context. When he flags a shift in the data (e.g., multiple compression, growth reacceleration), it often foreshadows broader market moves in software. He writes from the perspective of someone who bridges private and public markets, making his views on valuation particularly actionable.
