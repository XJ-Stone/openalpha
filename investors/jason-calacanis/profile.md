# Jason Calacanis

## Background
Jason Calacanis is a serial entrepreneur, angel investor, and media personality. He is best known as one of the earliest angel investors in Uber, an investment that reportedly returned over 5,000x. He founded LAUNCH, an early-stage venture fund and accelerator, and hosts This Week in Startups (TWIST), a long-running podcast covering the startup ecosystem. He is a co-host of the All-In Podcast alongside Chamath Palihapitiya, David Sacks, and David Friedberg.

## Investment Style
- Prolific angel investor focused on early-stage startups, with a portfolio of hundreds of investments
- Known for the "angel investing playbook": diversify broadly at the seed stage, then double down on winners
- Publicly stated on TWIST: "I want to find the next Uber — a company that creates an entirely new market" (paraphrased)
- Focuses on founder quality and market size above all else
- In public markets, tends to favor companies he understands as a user and operator
- Vocal advocate for startup-friendly policies and immigration reform for tech talent

## Current Known Positions (from public statements)
- **UBER (Uber)**: Iconic early investment. Continues to follow the company closely and comment on its autonomous vehicle strategy and marketplace dynamics.
- **TSLA (Tesla)**: Has discussed Tesla on All-In, generally positive on the long-term autonomous driving thesis.
- **NVDA (Nvidia)**: Bullish on Nvidia as the foundational AI infrastructure play.
- **GOOG (Alphabet)**: Has discussed Google's AI efforts and search dominance on multiple episodes.
- **RBLX (Roblox)**: Has discussed Roblox as a platform play for the next generation of internet users.

## How to Read Jason Calacanis
Calacanis is the most prolific content creator among the All-In hosts, producing both the weekly All-In Podcast and his own This Week in Startups show. His commentary style is more anecdotal and operator-focused than analytically driven. He often frames investment views through the lens of his personal experience as an entrepreneur and angel investor.

On All-In, he tends to play the role of the optimistic generalist, often championing startups and new technology. His public market views are less specific than Chamath's or Sacks', but his instincts on product-market fit and consumer adoption are informed by decades of early-stage investing. Pay particular attention when he draws parallels between current companies and his Uber experience — his pattern recognition for marketplace businesses and network effects is strong.
