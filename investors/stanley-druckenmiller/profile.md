# Stanley Druckenmiller

## Background
Stanley Druckenmiller is one of the most successful macro investors in history. He managed money for George Soros at Quantum Fund (famously helping engineer the 1992 British pound trade) before running Duquesne Capital Management, which he converted to a family office in 2010 after three decades of roughly 30% annual returns with no losing year. He is known for combining top-down macro analysis with concentrated, high-conviction positions and the willingness to size up aggressively when he has conviction.

## Investment Style
- Macro-first investor who uses top-down analysis to identify sectors and then picks the best companies within those sectors
- Famous for saying: "The way to build long-term returns is through preservation of capital and home runs"
- Willing to take very large positions (10-30% of portfolio) when conviction is high
- Adjusts positions rapidly — known for completely reversing positions based on new data
- Has described his edge as "being able to see 18 months out and then betting big when the odds are in my favor" (paraphrased from conference remarks)
- In recent years, has been selectively involved in technology, particularly AI infrastructure

## Current Known Positions (from 13F filings and public statements)
- **NVDA (Nvidia)**: Has publicly discussed owning Nvidia. Notably trimmed the position in mid-2024, stating at the Sohn Conference that he still liked Nvidia long-term but that the "easy money" had been made. Was early to identify Nvidia as an AI beneficiary.
- **MSFT (Microsoft)**: Has held Microsoft as a core AI position, viewing it as the enterprise distribution platform for AI.
- **CORT (Corcept Therapeutics)**: Position visible in 13F filings. A healthcare position reflecting his willingness to invest outside tech.
- **TJX (TJX Companies)**: Has held as a consumer position, reflecting his macro view on consumer spending patterns.

## How to Read Stanley Druckenmiller
Druckenmiller is selective in his public appearances, making each one more significant. He typically speaks at major investment conferences (Sohn Conference, Delivering Alpha, Robin Hood Conference) and occasionally does Bloomberg or CNBC interviews. His 13F filings are closely watched by institutional investors for position changes.

His comments tend to be macro-focused — interest rates, fiscal policy, currency dynamics — with specific stock picks anchored within those macro views. When he discusses a stock publicly, it often reflects a high-conviction, well-researched position. His willingness to publicly discuss trimming or exiting positions (as he did with Nvidia) makes him unusually transparent for a macro investor. Pay close attention to his comments on positioning and risk management, as his framework for sizing positions is central to his investment philosophy.
